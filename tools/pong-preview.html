Palette budget — 16 slots, global to the monitor

10 spent, 6 free. Pong redefines nothing — every slot is its CC stock colour. That is the direction, not an accident: the slot is a blue→purple animated gradient and the cage is a green→gold one, so pong's signature is that it has no gradient at all. A flat black court is what keeps the ball legible — see the note below.

Why the court must be flat black

A 2×2 ball straddles up to four cells. On a gradient court each of those cells would hold ball-white + two neighbouring band colours = 3 colours, and encodeCell collapses the extras into the background — the ball splinters into slivers exactly the way the slot's bulbs did (kb/monitor-ui.md). On a flat court there are only ever two colours in play, so the ball, the paddles and the net survive whole at every sub-cell position. The cheapest-looking choice is the only correct one.

Touch rectangles — cell coords, source of truth

Hit-testing is built against these now, ahead of the art pass. The art pass changes how things are drawn, never where they are touched.

Every rect is an exact mirror about the net (col c ↔ col 58-c). Nothing is asymmetric. The two GO rects are byte-identical by intent — lobby and results share one constant so the rematch button is literally the same button in the same place.

One bevel ramp: steel

white 240 / lightGray 153 / gray 76 — the only true 3-step ramp in the stock 16. Shared by both READY buttons and GO. READY off = raised steel. READY on = sunken lime — a latch should look pushed in, and depth + colour are two independent signals. GO inert = flat gray plate, no bevel, native word WAITING. GO live = steel bevel, yellow face, pixelfont GO. Different shape, colour, word and font — four signals on the one button that moves money.
